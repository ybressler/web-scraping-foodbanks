We bring nourishing food to the people of our region who have the need, and courage, to seek help. Working hand in hand with retailers, manufacturers, farmers, buying alliances and individual donors, our mission is to end hunger for every person in our region, today. And tomorrow, do it again.

play
Caring for kids

Hunger affects how kids learn and develop. We help kids grow up healthy.
support for seniors

Many seniors face a choice: buy food, or pay living expenses. We help take these hard decisions off the table.
emergency response

In times of personal crisis as well as city-wide disasters, we help families get the food they need.
distribution network

More than 300 partner agencies work with us to relieve hunger.
mobile delivery

For those who lack access to transportation as well as nutritious food, our delivery programs can help.
How we source food

With the help of retailers, manufacturers, farmers and buying alliances, we source 12 million pounds of food every year.

HullaBOWLoo 2020 - POSTPONED
16-May-2020
Chattanooga Area Food Bank Warehouse
2009 Curtain Pole Road, Chattanooga, TN 37406
5:30 PM - 11:30 PM

In response to the CDCs guidance to limit large events and in the interest of our communitys safety, HullaBOWLoo is postponed until fall 2020. We look forward to announcing a new date soon!

 
Join us for a taste of Chattanooga from local restaurants, breweries and distilleries. Proceeds will help to fill the bowls of those facing hunger in our region.

VIP Garden Party 5:30 - 6:30 p.m.  | Main Event 6:30 - 11 p.m.

Featuring entertainment by Priscila and Little RicKee, Rising Fawn Social Club and Isaac White.  Late Night Dance Party by DJ Reece Varnell. Silent and live auctions.
 
To learn more or to sponsor, please contact Shelley Armstrong, Director of Events and Major Gifts, 423-622-1800 x226 or sarmstrong@chattfoodbank.org.

21 and older event.

OPERATION FEED OUR NEIGHBORS

The Chattanooga Area Food Bank anticipates an additional cost of up to $100,000 every month to cover the growing need caused by the coronavirus and the economic impact that will follow.If major food donors like manufacturers or grocers are unable to continue donating food, our costs will grow.

As you see pictures of empty store shelves, remember 1 in 8 people overall including 1 in 5 children face hunger in Southeast Tennessee and Northwest Georgia. Their pantry shelves may look like this every day. Stocking up isnt possible for them when putting food on the table is a daily struggle. Join the Chattanooga Area Food Bank and give to Operation Feed Our Neighbors to help children, families, seniors and others in need.

Donate: Every $1 helps to provide 4 meals.
